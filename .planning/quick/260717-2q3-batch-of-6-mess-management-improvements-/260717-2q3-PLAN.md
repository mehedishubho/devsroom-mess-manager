    Locked decision applies: investigate first, fix what is actually broken, do not blindly mutate. Per grounded recon:

    1. Root-cause analysis (then choose ONE fix):
       The current `_confirmation-modal.blade.php` puts `@click.outside="open = false"` on the INNER white panel (`<div class="w-full max-w-md ...">`). Alpine v3's `@click.outside` is known to fire on the same DOM event that opened the element under certain timing conditions (when the open-trigger click bubbles to document after the listener is attached). When this happens, opening the modal immediately closes it; subsequent attempts can leave the modal in an inconsistent state. Additionally, when a user clicks the Cancel button inside the panel, the click DOES originate inside the panel — so `@click.outside` should NOT fire. The most likely user-visible bug is therefore either:
       (a) the modal opens then immediately closes (open trigger swallowed), OR
       (b) the modal Cancel button's `@click="open = false"` works but the user reports the modal re-appearing on the next click of the trigger because Alpine's `x-show` transition state is stale.

    2. Apply the standard, well-tested fix: MOVE `@click.outside="open = false"` OFF the inner panel and ONTO the OUTER backdrop (`<div class="fixed inset-0 z-50 ...">`). The outer backdrop covers the viewport; clicking anywhere outside the white panel (i.e. on the dark backdrop) closes the modal — desired UX. The Cancel button's existing `@click="open = false"` then works without interference.

    3. Also remove `@click.outside` from the inner panel entirely. The inner panel keeps no outside-click handler.

    4. Keep the existing `@keydown.escape.window="open = false"` on the outer backdrop (Escape still closes — desired).

    5. Verify the modal markup and the trigger button share the SAME Alpine `x-data` scope.
